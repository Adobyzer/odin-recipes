To warm ourselves with a small glass of NahNah liqueur.

Description

"Green tea with mint. Moroccan. Traditional tea from North Africa."

An excellent beverage to recall the heat of the Sahara and aid in digesting the incoming food!

Ingredients

  • Water (500 ml)
  • Green Tea (2 cups)
  • Sugar (2 px)
  • Fresh Mint (1 px)

Steps

  1. Wash and carefully dry the bunch of fresh mint. Gently pluck the leaves.
  2. In a saucepan, bring the 500ml of water to a boil.
  3. Place the green tea in a bowl, then pour a glass of boiling water over it.
  4. Let it steep for 5 minutes, discard the infused liquid while retaining the green tea (this helps remove the initial bitterness of the tea).
  5. Place the green tea in a teapot.
  6. Pour the remaining boiling water over the green tea.
  7. Add the mint leaves followed by the sugar cubes.
  8. mix your Moroccan mint tea, do not use a spoon: pour the tea into a glass and then pour it back into the teapot. Repeat the process several times to ensure the tea is well blended.
  9. Serve your Moroccan mint tea very hot, holding the teapot high above the glasses to create a "foam" with the sugar.

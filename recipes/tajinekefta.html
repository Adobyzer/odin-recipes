Description

"This kefta and egg tagine, enhanced with olives, is a daily dish in Algeria, Morroco and Tunisia."

Nothing better than a well-rounded tagine accompanied by everything we love. This dish can also be enjoyed without using utensils, just with some warm bread.

Ingredients

  • Minced beef (600 g)
  • Onions (2 px)
  • Tomato (5 px)
  • Paprika (2 px)
  • Garlic (1 px)
  • Eggs (4 px)
  • Ginger (2 cups)

Steps

  1. For making your kefta and egg tagine, start by preparing the minced beef meatballs. Finely chop 1 onion, add the spices: 1 tsp of cumin, paprika, and ginger, along with parsley, and mix with the minced beef. Form your meatballs.
  2. Put a few drops of olive oil in your tagine dish, heat it, and add your meatballs. Brown them for just a few minutes, then remove them from the dish.
  3. In the tagine, add a previously chopped onion, 1 tsp each of cumin, paprika, and ginger, crushed garlic, and quartered tomatoes. Cook this sauce for 15 minutes over low heat, stirring occasionally with a wooden spoon. Return the minced beef meatballs to the tagine and sprinkle with olives. Cover and continue cooking over low heat for 10 to 15 minutes.
  4. You can then crack your eggs into the dish, cover, and let them cook for another two to three minutes. Chop parsley to garnish your tagine.
